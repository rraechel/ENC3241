# Proposal

The Rise of Online Gambling Activity and Addiction Research
					 

Background

Gambling has become more accessible in everyone’s lives and is recognized as a serious addiction for some. With the rise of popularity, new gambling platforms are being created at a high rate. Online gambling has allowed for the ability to place a bet from your electronic devices at any time with ease. 
After examining the results of multiple psychological studies, there is evidence that shows online betting platforms are contributing to the rise of gambling addictions. According to the study, participants that were deemed a higher risk for gambling harm showed to be more likely to make a deposit to qualify for a marketing offer. This study also showed that both higher-risk and lower-risk participants were more likely to place a risky bet when using bonus funds (Farrell).
A study researching the impacts of gambling advertisements shows that gambling advertisements influences gambling behavior and drives their gambling activity (Díaz-Bustamante Ventisca). Over 80% of participants reported that gambling advertisements are effective due to the encouragement. These advertisements target the vulnerable population in the audience who may not understand the risks of gambling and signs of addiction. 
Another study was done during the 2022 FIFA World Cup, a large sporting event that was streamed by over a billion people. The study focused on advertisements and the effects they have on gambling behaviors. The results show that the advertisements did increase betting during live sports events (McGrane). The results also show this increase despite the restrictions placed on gambling advertisements during live sports events. 
Based on the results of these studies, gambling addiction is on the rise. This rise is largely due to the huge number of online platforms offered, advertisements prompting the behavior, and the encouraging offers from platforms to new gamblers and current ones.

Methodology 

For the study of the increase in gambling addictions and activity, a study will be conducted with volunteers as participants. The study will be conducted with no more than 35 participants.  
The participants will first receive a questionnaire that analyzes their current gambling behaviors. This will help the researchers gather an understanding of participants history and background knowledge of gambling. The second part of the study will include another questionnaire sent out to these participants after a large, televised sporting event. 
The second questionnaire will gather information on the participants gambling behaviors now. Questions will include whether they watched recent sporting event, what platform they used to stream it, and if they saw any gambling advertisements. Participants will also be asked if they received any sports betting offers during a live event if they are users of any gambling platforms, and whether they placed a bet because of the offer. 
Anticipated Outcomes  
This research will allow an understanding of the relationship between gambling advertisements, gambling platforms offers, and the audience of sports events. These results will be able to show if there is a relationship between the rise of online gambling activity and addiction and gambling platforms use of marketing. 


Significance 

If there are significant findings showing a strong correlation between the two, it will result in the ability to push for strict advertising regulation and restrictions. Educating the population on the risks of gambling will also be seen as vital. Furthermore, encouraging the option to place restrictions on a viewer’s own advertising settings should be taken into consideration. Allowing for viewers to block gambling advertisements from their streaming platforms will decrease the harm in the high-risk population.
